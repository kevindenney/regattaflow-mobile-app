# Battle Card 1: vs SAILTI

## Competitor Overview

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Company** | SAILTI (sailti.com) |
| **HQ** | Spain |
| **Founded** | ~2010 |
| **Primary Market** | Spanish-speaking countries, international classes |
| **Key Clients** | RFEV, RYA, FFVoile, 470 Class, IODA, iQFoil |

## Their Strengths

✅ Established federation relationships  
✅ Complete suite (Scoring, Jury, Race Signals, Web)  
✅ Proven at major international championships  
✅ Multi-language support  
✅ Strong in Olympic classes  

## Their Weaknesses

❌ No AI features (race strategy, coaching)  
❌ Aging technology stack  
❌ Mobile experience is web-based, not native  
❌ Limited offline capability  
❌ Spanish-centric support  
❌ No Sailwave integration  
❌ No 3D visualization or tactical maps  

## Our Advantages

| Feature | SAILTI | RegattaFlow |
|---------|--------|-------------|
| AI Race Strategy | ❌ None | ✅ 15+ Claude skills |
| AI Coaching | ❌ None | ✅ Post-race analysis |
| 3D Bathymetry | ❌ None | ✅ Interactive maps |
| Mobile App | Web-based | ✅ Native iOS/Android |
| Offline Mode | Limited | ✅ Full offline-first |
| Sailwave Integration | Basic export | ✅ Full import/export |
| Weather Intelligence | Basic | ✅ Open-Meteo + AI |

## Key Differentiator Message

> "SAILTI is great for running the operational side of a regatta. RegattaFlow does that AND gives your sailors something SAILTI can't - personalized AI race strategy and coaching. We're not just race management; we're sailing intelligence."

## Competitive Positioning

```
"SAILTI manages your regatta. 
RegattaFlow makes your sailors faster."
```

## When You'll Win

✅ Prospect values AI/innovation  
✅ Sailors are key stakeholders (not just race committee)  
✅ Mobile experience is a priority  
✅ They want Sailwave compatibility  
✅ Non-Spanish speaking organization  
✅ Looking for a modern, API-first platform  

## When You'll Lose

❌ Deep existing SAILTI relationship  
❌ Spanish federation (SAILTI home market)  
❌ Need World Sailing certification (they have it, we don't yet)  
❌ Very large event with no tolerance for new platform risk  

## Objection Handling

**"We've used SAILTI for years and it works"**
> "That makes sense - they've been around a long time. What we hear from classes who've tried both is that SAILTI handles the operations well, but their sailors love what RegattaFlow adds on top - the AI strategy, the native app experience, the tactical insights. Many use both: SAILTI for scoring, RegattaFlow for the sailor experience."

**"SAILTI has federation relationships"**
> "They do, and we respect that. Our approach is different - we work with classes and clubs first, prove value, then expand. We're not asking you to replace SAILTI overnight. Start with a pilot, see what your sailors think."

**"Can you handle [major championship]?"**
> "We're focused on getting it right before going big. We'd rather do an amazing job at a mid-size event than stumble at a worlds. Let's prove ourselves at [smaller event] first."

## Trap Questions to Ask

- "How do your sailors prepare for races currently?"
- "What do competitors say about the mobile experience?"
- "Do you offer any coaching or learning tools?"
- "How do clubs that use Sailwave integrate with your system?"

# Battle Card 2: vs Sailwave

## Competitor Overview

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Software** | Sailwave (sailwave.com) |
| **Type** | Desktop application (Windows) |
| **Cost** | Free (donationware) |
| **Market Share** | Dominant at club level worldwide |
| **Developer** | Colin Jenkins (UK) |

## Their Strengths

✅ **Free** - hard to compete on price  
✅ Incredibly powerful scoring engine  
✅ Industry standard file format (.BLW)  
✅ Huge install base and familiarity  
✅ Handles complex scoring scenarios  
✅ Active development and support forums  

## Their Weaknesses

❌ Desktop-only (Windows)  
❌ No mobile app  
❌ No online registration  
❌ No real-time results for spectators  
❌ Dated user interface  
❌ No AI or intelligence features  
❌ No competitor/sailor features  
❌ Manual result publishing workflow  

## Our Position: Complement, Not Compete

**Critical**: We do NOT position against Sailwave. We position as the **companion** that adds what Sailwave lacks.

## Key Message

> "Keep using Sailwave - it's great at scoring. Use RegattaFlow for everything else: mobile apps, online registration, live results, AI coaching. Import and export .BLW files seamlessly."

## Integration Pitch

```
Sailwave (Scoring) + RegattaFlow (Everything Else) = Complete Solution

- Race Committee: Score in Sailwave as usual
- Export results to RegattaFlow with one click
- Competitors get: Mobile app, push notifications, AI strategy
- Spectators get: Live results, standings, updates
```

## Feature Comparison (Positioning as Complementary)

| Capability | Sailwave | RegattaFlow | Together |
|------------|----------|-------------|----------|
| Scoring Engine | ✅ Excellent | ✅ Good | Use Sailwave |
| Mobile App | ❌ None | ✅ Native | RegattaFlow |
| Online Registration | ❌ None | ✅ Yes | RegattaFlow |
| Live Results | ❌ None | ✅ Yes | RegattaFlow |
| AI Strategy | ❌ None | ✅ Yes | RegattaFlow |
| Offline Desktop | ✅ Yes | ❌ Web/Mobile | Sailwave |
| Complex Handicaps | ✅ Excellent | 🟡 Basic | Use Sailwave |

## When to Use This Card

- Prospect says "our clubs use Sailwave"
- Race officers are resistant to change
- There's no budget for new tools
- Club-level organizations

## Objection Handling

**"Our race officers won't change from Sailwave"**
> "They don't have to! That's the beauty of our approach. Race officers continue using Sailwave exactly as they do now. They export a .BLW file, import to RegattaFlow, and suddenly competitors have a mobile app with live results and AI coaching. Zero change to the race officer workflow."

**"Sailwave is free"**
> "Sailwave is fantastic value - we encourage clubs to keep using it for scoring. RegattaFlow adds the mobile and AI layer. Our club tier is €500/year, which gets you online registration, native apps, and features Sailwave simply can't offer as a desktop tool."

**"We don't need another system"**
> "I hear you. Think of us as an extension of Sailwave, not a replacement. Your race officers keep their workflow. Your competitors get a 21st-century experience. One .BLW import and you're connected."

## Partnership Opportunity

Consider reaching out to Colin Jenkins (Sailwave developer) about:
- Official "companion app" status
- Listing on Sailwave Third Party Resources page
- Joint promotion to Sailwave user base

# Battle Card 5: vs "Do Nothing" / Status Quo

## The Real Competitor

Often, the biggest competitor isn't another platform - it's inertia. "We've always done it this way."

## Status Quo Components

- **Sailwave** for scoring (desktop)
- **Email** for notices and communication
- **Club website** for results posting
- **WhatsApp groups** for updates
- **Spreadsheets** for registration

## Pain Points of Status Quo

| Pain | Impact |
|------|--------|
| Manual result uploads | Race officer time wasted |
| No push notifications | Competitors miss updates |
| Fragmented communication | Confusion, missed notices |
| No mobile experience | Poor competitor satisfaction |
| No AI coaching | Missed learning opportunity |
| No spectator engagement | Lower event profile |

## Our Value Proposition

> "You can keep doing what you're doing. But your competitors expect more - they have live scores for football, why not sailing? Your race officers deserve less admin work. Your sailors deserve AI coaching. And you deserve a platform that just works."

## ROI Arguments

**For Race Officers:**
- Automated result publishing saves 2+ hours per event
- Push notifications eliminate "did they see it?" anxiety
- Sailwave integration means no workflow change

**For Competitors:**
- AI race strategy (unique value)
- Mobile app with offline (reliability)
- Live results (engagement)

**For Class/Club:**
- Professional image
- Competitor satisfaction
- Data and analytics
- Sponsor visibility

## Key Questions to Surface Pain

- "How long does it take to publish results after each race?"
- "How do competitors find out about schedule changes?"
- "What do your sailors use to prepare for races?"
- "How do spectators follow the racing?"
- "What feedback do you get from competitors about the technology experience?"

# Battle Card 6: vs Custom/DIY Solutions

## The Competitor

Some organizations build their own solutions:
- Custom websites
- Bespoke apps
- Integrated WordPress plugins
- Developer-maintained systems

## Why They Built Custom

- "Commercial solutions didn't fit"
- "We have technical volunteers"
- "We wanted control"
- "Cost seemed lower"

## Hidden Costs of DIY

| Cost | Often Overlooked |
|------|------------------|
| Maintenance | Ongoing developer time |
| Updates | OS changes, security patches |
| Mobile | Expensive to maintain two platforms |
| Features | Each new feature = development |
| Support | Who helps when it breaks? |
| Scaling | Can it handle growth? |

## Our Value Proposition

> "Building your own made sense when commercial options didn't exist. Now you're spending volunteer time maintaining software instead of running sailing. Let us handle the technology; you focus on the sport."

## Key Questions

- "How much time does your technical team spend on maintenance?"
- "What happens if your developer volunteer moves away?"
- "When did you last add a significant new feature?"
- "Could you add AI coaching to your system?"

## Migration Path

We offer free data migration from custom systems:
1. Export competitor history
2. Map data to RegattaFlow schema
3. Import and verify
4. Run parallel for one event
5. Full cutover
