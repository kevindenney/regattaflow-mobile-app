The rule on general recalls affects one’s tactics with
regard to where on the line to start. It reads, “... when after
a general recall, any part of a yacht’s hull, crew or equip-
ment is on the course side of the starting line or its exten-
sions during the minute before her starting signal, she
shall return to the pre-start side of the line across one of
its extensions and start.” General recalls occur when so
many boats are over the line early the race committee is
unable to identify them all. The only fair thing is to have
another start. To reduce the possibility of a second, third,
or even fourth general recall in a particularly aggressive
fleet, the rule above was devised. Commonly known as the
